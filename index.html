A hero—but not like the kind in books and movies

In this case, our hero is the first thing visitors see when they land on a web page. Its job is simple: grab attention, communicate value, and encourage action. A strong hero section combines clear messaging with compelling visuals—whether that's an image, illustration, video, infographic, or form—to guide visitors toward the next step.

People are naturally skeptical. Social proof helps build trust by showing that others have already benefited from what you're offering. Testimonials, reviews, customer counts, and case studies all serve the same purpose: reducing uncertainty and increasing confidence.

Consider highlighting a client testimonial, perhaps. You could also share a relevant thought, idea, or quotation. One might even employ a mantra or mission statement, even. Many landing pages include a testimonial at this point. Rather than inventing one, this placeholder is serving as an example.
